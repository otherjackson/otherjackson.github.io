Today was a day of meetings, the most interesting of which was with Thiago. Of course I've been throwing spaghetti at the wall to see what sticks with Solid, but he helped me realize a few paths that can be taken. Self-sovereign identity (the system behind COVID verification), needs to be top down. It needs government and large organization backing. It's a large problem that if solved, would be profoundly impactful, but I don't see it working from a grassroots perspective because self-sovereign doesn't work unless governments and large organizations buy into a single standard. There may be an opportunity to do this with Solid because it already has support of the UK and Belgian governments, but to be sure, it would be great to have someone involved who has the connections to really push it. So, if we go the self Self-sovereign route, I'll need to ask for help in that department. We'd also be competing with a company called Bindle.

Alternatively, there's the killer app for Solid that can be a bit more grassroots. Making a data management platform that is extremely user friendly might be the way to go. It would be similar to ShapeRepo but targeted at a non-developer user-base. There's a rise in applications that allow users to basically build their own applications (things like retool and airtable). I could imagine a Solid data management platform that lets people easily set up new "apps" for organizing their own data and a community's data. For example, I've seen so many startups proposed that are along the lines of "Connect X with Y" as in "Connect people selling textbooks with people who want to buy textbooks." And we don't need a new app for that. We can set a data standard for it and let a generic UI render it.

I'll definitely be taking some of these insights into my meeting with Ann on Friday.
